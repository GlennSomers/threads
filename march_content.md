## Newsletters (4)

**Newsletter 1**
Subject: Why you keep saying you'll start again soon
The "I'll shoot this weekend" loop. What keeps people from returning after a gap — the specific friction between intending to go out and actually going out with the camera. The 2-3 year absence is the entry point. The newsletter is about what stopping actually felt like and what broke the loop, not a recovery narrative.

**Newsletter 2**
Subject: The photo that stayed an idea
The perfectionism mechanism in detail. How keeping the photo as an idea prevents it from failing, and why a failed attempt at a great photo feels worse than no attempt at all, until it doesn't. There's a specific session where this shifted. That session is the spine of it.

**Newsletter 3**
Subject: The editing sequence most people get backwards
The exposure baseline problem. Why beginners spend an hour fighting their own edits because the foundation was never right to begin with. The actual sequence: histogram check, baseline, then shape. The payoff is understanding the why. Natural home for a preset mention.

**Newsletter 4**
Subject: What "enough gear" actually looks like
The 1300-euro setup. What it does, what it can't, and why the ceiling matters less than most people assume when they're starting out. An honest account of what upgrading actually changes and what stays the same regardless.
