## MINDSET

spend enough time in camera forums and the next upgrade starts to feel like the only thing standing between you and better photos. what gets said less is that the camera you already own can take those photos, and the gap closes with more shooting, not different gear.

---

## TACTILE TIP

when edits feel inconsistent the instinct is to reach for the colour sliders. starting there is usually the wrong move. if the exposure baseline isn't clean, colour adjustments land on top of the real problem rather than fixing it. open the histogram first, nail the exposure, shape the contrast — colour tends to fall in line from there.

## PERSONAL INSIGHT

for a long time i convinced myself that putting the camera down was a loss of interest, but that wasn't right. i just ran out of patience with being bad at photography, and picking the camera up had become a reminder of how far back i still was. coming back wasn't some moment of clarity, i just picked it up again one day and kept shooting badly for a while longer, which as far as i can tell is still the only way through.

## DEBUNKING

a high megapixel count doesn't fix bad light. a technically clean shot of a bad scene is still a bad photo.

## NEWSLETTER OPENER

"just keep shooting" is the standard advice for flat sessions. push through, stay consistent, the habit is what matters. i followed that for a long time and kept showing up to sessions where nothing was working, grinding through them and telling myself the flat days were just part of the process. what i worked out much later is that i wasn't building anything useful in those sessions. mostly i was just getting slowly worse at wanting to pick the camera up.

the eye doesn't disappear when a session goes flat, it just isn't there that day, and there are usually pretty ordinary reasons why. sleep, what you've been watching, what kind of week it's been — all of these feed into whether the eye is on or off. once i started treating bad sessions as weather rather than proof that something was wrong, picking the camera up stopped feeling like a test i was about to fail.
